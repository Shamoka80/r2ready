Two distinct R2v3 training programs are required:

-----
**A. R2v3 Business Implementation Training (for facilities seeking certification)**

**Purpose:** Prepare company staff to achieve and maintain full R2v3 (v3.1) certification readiness.\
**Structure:** 10 Core Requirement Modules + 7 Appendix Modules + 4 Specialized Capstone Sessions.\
**Duration:** 24–30 hours (can be delivered as a 3–5 day intensive or modular online course).

-----
**MODULE 1 – R2v3 Overview & Certification Fundamentals**

- R2 history, purpose, and global context.
- Certification cycle: readiness → audit → certification → surveillance.
- Core vs. Appendix structure (Core + applicable Appendices).
- R2v3 v3.1 updates (2024 revision, PV modules inclusion).
- Roles: facility, certification body, consultant, and SERI.
-----
**MODULE 2 – Core Requirement 1: Scope Definition**

- Determining certification boundaries and facility scope.
- Multi-site and campus certification models.
- Required documentation and legal entity alignment.
- Exercise: Define your facility’s scope and controlled streams.
-----
**MODULE 3 – Core Requirement 2: Hierarchy of Responsible Management**

- Reuse-first hierarchy and downstream decision-making.
- Reuse vs. recovery vs. disposal pathways.
- Decision tree development for handling streams.
- Practical exercise: mapping your process hierarchy.
-----
**MODULE 4 – Core Requirement 3: Environmental, Health & Safety Management System (EHSMS)**

- Integration of ISO 14001, ISO 45001, or RIOS.
- Building EHSMS documentation and procedures.
- Risk assessments and worker safety controls.
- Annual internal audit and management review requirements.
-----
**MODULE 5 – Core Requirement 4: Legal and Other Requirements**

- Building and maintaining a legal compliance register.
- Import/export legal verification procedures.
- Environmental permits and reporting requirements.
- Ethical labor, anti-discrimination, and fair-practice policies.
-----
**MODULE 6 – Core Requirement 5: Tracking Throughput**

- Recordkeeping for inbound/outbound streams.
- Inventory limits and storage duration rules.
- Documentation templates: bills of lading, summaries, chain-of-custody.
- Digital systems for throughput tracking.
-----
**MODULE 7 – Core Requirement 6: Sorting, Categorization, and Processing**

- R2 Equipment Categorization (REC) usage and correlation.
- Practical classification of equipment and Focus Materials.
- Handling prohibited items and reuse qualification.
- Case study: Applying REC mapping to your facility.
-----
**MODULE 8 – Core Requirement 7: Data Security**

- Data Sanitization Plan and secured area setup.
- Logical vs. physical data sanitization (Appendix B tie-in).
- Worker authorization and training requirements.
- Supplier communication and incident response planning.
-----
**MODULE 9 – Core Requirement 8: Focus Materials**

- Identifying and managing mercury, batteries, PCBs, CRTs, circuit boards, solar cells.
- Developing an FM Management Plan and flowchart.
- Downstream management and verification.
- Integration into the EHSMS.
-----
**MODULE 10 – Core Requirements 9–10: Facility & Transport Controls**

- Facility storage and environmental protection standards.
- Closure plan, financial assurance, and insurance requirements.
- Secure and compliant transport practices.
- Audit evidence preparation and mock documentation.
-----
**MODULES 11–17 – Appendices A–G**

Each appendix as a self-contained unit with “proof of compliance” workshops:

1. **A: Downstream Recycling Chain** – vendor qualification and chain transparency.
1. **B: Data Sanitization** – hands-on data destruction and logging practice.
1. **C: Test and Repair** – building and validating a Reuse Plan and QMS linkage.
1. **D: Specialty Electronics Reuse** – verified specialty equipment procedures.
1. **E: Materials Recovery** – risk assessment and Focus Material removal.
1. **F: Brokering** – traceability when handling no physical materials.
1. **G: Photovoltaic Modules** – handling, testing, and EHS controls for PVs.
-----
**MODULE 18 – Internal Auditing & Gap Closure**

- Conducting internal mock audits.
- Identifying non-conformances and corrective actions.
- Preparing documentation for certification audits.
- Use of R2v3 compliance checklist and readiness scoring.
-----
**MODULE 19 – Continuous Improvement & Surveillance Maintenance**

- Annual surveillance audits and document updates.
- Continuous improvement logs and change management.
- Staff competency retention and training refresh cycles.
-----
**MODULE 20 – Digital Tools & Reporting**

- Using readiness assessment tools (e.g., R2Ready app).
- Report generation and record retention best practices.
- Automation and dashboard integration.
-----
**Deliverables for Business Trainees**

- Sample policies and templates for each Core and Appendix.
- Internal audit checklist.
- FM Management Plan example.
- Mock assessment exercise (graded).
- Certificate of Completion (R2v3 Business Implementation).
-----
**B. R2v3 Auditor/Agency Training (for certification bodies, consultants, or agents)**

**Purpose:** Ensure auditors, CB staff, and consulting agents perform accurate and consistent R2v3 audits per SERI Code of Practices.\
**Structure:** 8 technical modules + 4 professional practice modules.\
**Duration:** 20–25 hours.

-----
**MODULE 1 – R2v3 Governance & SERI Framework**

- SERI, TAC, and Code of Practices overview.
- Accreditation and CB oversight process.
- R2v3 version history and v3.1 updates.
-----
**MODULE 2 – Certification Process Lifecycle**

- Application review → document review → Stage 1 → Stage 2 audit.
- Certification, surveillance, and recertification cycles.
- Scope statements and multi-site certification evaluation.
- Audit evidence standards and sampling rules.
-----
**MODULE 3 – Core Requirement Audit Deep Dive (1–10)**

- How to test conformance and what constitutes objective evidence.
- Common nonconformities and risk indicators per section.
- Record sampling and verification criteria.
-----
**MODULE 4 – Appendix Audit Deep Dive (A–G)**

- How to determine applicability and audit boundaries.
- Data sanitization verification (Appendix B focus).
- Downstream vendor audit and sampling verification.
- Specialty reuse and PV module auditing best practices.
-----
**MODULE 5 – REC Mapping & Technical Evaluation**

- Using REC documentation during audits.
- Verifying categorization consistency and traceability.
- Interview and observation techniques.
-----
**MODULE 6 – Legal, EHS, and Data Security Verification**

- Evaluating legal compliance registers and permits.
- Reviewing FM Management Plans and downstream flowcharts.
- Data security walkthroughs and chain-of-custody testing.
-----
**MODULE 7 – Scoring, Reporting, and Nonconformance Management**

- Major vs. Minor nonconformance definitions.
- Root cause and corrective action evaluation.
- Evidence documentation and audit report writing.
-----
**MODULE 8 – Auditor Ethics & Impartiality**

- Conflict-of-interest prevention.
- Confidentiality, impartiality, and professional conduct.
- SERI’s enforcement and appeal mechanisms.
-----
**MODULE 9 – Audit Simulation Workshop**

- Full mock facility audit with scoring.
- Writing findings, evidence logs, and final summary.
- Peer review and calibration exercise.
-----
**MODULE 10 – Continuous Calibration & Updates**

- Interpreting SERI Formal Interpretations and Guidance updates.
- Annual requalification process.
- Incorporating changes to ISO standards (9001, 14001, 45001).
-----
**Deliverables for Auditor/Agency Trainees**

- R2v3 Audit Workbook (based on Compliance Checklist).
- Example audit plan, sampling sheet, and scoring log.
- SERI Code of Practices summary.
- Certificate of Competency (R2v3 Auditor/Agency).
-----
